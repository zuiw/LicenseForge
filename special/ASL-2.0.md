## Preamble

The ASL-2.0 addresses the unique safety and accountability requirements of
autonomous systems — self-driving vehicles, autonomous drones, robotics,
and AI agents operating without direct human control.  It requires safety
case documentation, operational domain definition, mandatory incident
reporting, and fail-safe mechanisms.

"Incident" means any situation where the system causes or nearly causes

### 2. Safety Case.

Before deploying the Software in an autonomous system, you must develop a
Safety Case that includes:
- **a)** The defined Operational Domain;
- **b)** Hazard analysis and risk assessment;
- **c)** Safety requirements and verification;
- **d)** Testing and validation results.

### 3. Operational Domain.

The autonomous system must operate only within its defined Operational
Domain.  If the system exits the Operational Domain, it must:
- **a)** Detect the exit reliably;
- **b)** Alert the operator or passengers;
- **c)** Transition to a safe state.

### 4. Incident Reporting.

Any Incident must be:
- **a)** Logged with full telemetry data;
- **b)** Reported to the copyright holder within 7 days;
- **c)** Analyzed and documented with corrective actions.

### 5. Fail-Safe.

The system must have a fail-safe state that it can reach without causing
harm, triggered by:
- System failure or malfunction;
- Loss of communication;
- Operational Domain exit.
