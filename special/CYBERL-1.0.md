## Preamble

The CYBERL governs software for cyber-physical systems where computation interacts directly with the physical world: industrial control, automotive, medical devices, and critical infrastructure. It requires safety certification to applicable standards, documented real-time guarantees, and enforcement of physical safety boundaries.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"CPS" means a system integrating computation, networking, and physical processes.

"Safety Boundary" means a physical constraint enforced by software (maximum speed, temperature, current, etc.).

"Real-Time Guarantee" means a deterministic upper bound on computation or communication latency.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the CPS conditions below.

### 2. Safety Certification.

The Software must be certified to applicable safety standards (IEC 61508, ISO 26262, etc.) for its intended domain.

### 3. Real-Time Disclosure.

All Real-Time Guarantees, including worst-case execution time, must be documented.

### 4. Safety Boundary Enforcement.

Safety Boundaries must be implemented in hardware-enforced layers, not solely in software.

### 5. Fail-Safe.

The Software must include documented fail-safe states and behavior on system failure.
