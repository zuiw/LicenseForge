## Preamble

The FCL addresses the sustainability crisis in open source.  Many companies
profit from open-source software without contributing back to its
maintainers.  This license requires that if you generate revenue above a
specified threshold using the Software, you must provide fair compensation
to the maintainers, either through direct payment or equivalent support.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Fair Compensation License
(FCL-1.0).

"The Software" means the project licensed under this License.

"Revenue" means gross revenue derived directly from the Software, including
but not limited to SaaS fees, subscription fees, and paid support.

"Revenue Threshold" means the minimum Revenue amount specified in the
License notice that triggers compensation obligations.  If none is
specified, the threshold is $1,000,000 USD per calendar year.

"Fair Compensation" means either:
- **a)** A payment equal to 1% of Revenue exceeding the Revenue Threshold;
- **b)** The equivalent value in maintainer support, infrastructure, or
  services mutually agreed upon.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software for any lawful
purpose, subject to the fair compensation conditions below.

### 2. Compensation Obligation.

If your annual Revenue from the Software exceeds the Revenue Threshold, you
must provide Fair Compensation to the copyright holder within 120 days of
the end of the fiscal year.

### 3. Reporting.

If your Revenue exceeds 50% of the Revenue Threshold, you must:
- Provide an annual usage report to the copyright holder;
- Indicate whether the Revenue Threshold has been reached;
- Not disclose proprietary financial information beyond what is necessary.

### 4. Non-Profit Exemption.

Non-profit organizations, educational institutions, and government agencies
are exempt from the compensation obligation, but must still provide the
annual usage report if required above.

### 5. Audit.

The copyright holder may request a third-party audit of Revenue if they have
reasonable cause to believe compensation is owed.  Audit costs are borne by
the copyright holder unless underpayment of more than 10% is found.

### 6. Termination.

Failure to provide Fair Compensation when required terminates rights.
A 90-day cure period applies for a first violation.
