## Preamble

The HML ensures that humans always remain in control of critical decisions
when using AI-assisted systems.  It requires that AI recommendations be
clearly labeled as such, that humans can review and override AI decisions,
and that systems be designed for meaningful human control rather than
rubber-stamping.

### 0. Definitions.

(HML-1.0).

License.

"AI Recommendation" means any output from the Software that suggests a
decision, action, or judgment.

"Meaningful Human Control" means a human operator has sufficient
information, time, and authority to meaningfully evaluate and override AI
Recommendations before action is taken.

"Automated Decision" means a decision made by the Software without human
intervention at the point of decision.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software for any lawful

### 2. Human Oversight.

For any decision that has a significant impact on individuals, the Software
must:
- **a)** Present AI Recommendations with supporting rationale;
- **b)** Allow human operators to review and override decisions;
- **c)** Require human confirmation before executing consequential actions;
- **d)** Log all overrides and their justifications.

### 3. Transparency.

The Software must:
- **a)** Clearly label AI Recommendations as machine-generated;
- **b)** Disclose the confidence level or uncertainty of recommendations;
- **c)** Explain the factors that influenced each recommendation;
- **d)** Indicate when the system is operating outside its training
  distribution.

### 4. No Automation Bias.

The interface must be designed to reduce automation bias:
- Present alternatives when recommending a course of action;
- Avoid misleading confidence indicators;
- Support independent human reasoning before revealing AI suggestions.

### 5. Monitoring.

The Software must log:
- All AI Recommendations and whether they were accepted or overridden;
- Instances where human operators reversed AI decisions;
- System performance metrics for human oversight effectiveness.

### 6. Termination.

Deployment without Meaningful Human Control for consequential decisions
terminates rights.  A 60-day cure period applies.
