The GDTL governs software that processes genetic and genomic data. ### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Genetic Data Trust License (GDTL-1.0).

"The Software" means the genetic data processing, analysis, or storage system licensed under this License.

"Genetic Data" means DNA, RNA, gene expression, epigenetic, or other heritable biological sequence data.

"Data Subject" means the person whose Genetic Data is processed.

"Informed Consent" means explicit, specific, and revocable permission for specified processing of Genetic Data.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the genetic data conditions below.

### 2. Informed Consent.

Before processing Genetic Data, you must:
- **a)** Obtain Informed Consent from the Data Subject;
- **b)** Disclose exactly what analyses will be performed;
- **c)** Disclose who will have access to the data;
- **d)** Specify the duration of data retention.

### 3. Non-Discrimination.

You must not use Genetic Data to:
- **a)** Deny health insurance coverage;
- **b)** Make employment decisions;
- **c)** Discriminate in education, housing, or credit;
- **d)** Restrict access to services based on genetic traits.

### 4. Data Subject Rights.

Data Subjects have the right to:
- **a)** Access their Genetic Data in a portable format;
- **b)** Request correction of errors;
- **c)** Withdraw consent and request deletion;
- **d)** Know who has accessed their data.

### 5. Security.

Genetic Data must be:
- **a)** Encrypted at rest and in transit;
- **b)** Stored with access controls and audit logging;
- **c)** De-identified for research use where feasible;
- **d)** Protected against re-identification attacks.

### 6. Termination.

Processing Genetic Data without consent or using it for discrimination terminates rights. No cure period for discrimination violations.
