## Preamble

The RFL addresses the societal impact of AI systems.  It requires that AI
systems built using the Software undergo bias testing, provide transparency
reports, maintain human oversight for high-stakes decisions, and respect
user autonomy.  It is designed for machine learning frameworks, AI
deployment platforms, and decision support systems.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Responsible AI License
(RFL-1.0).

"The Software" means the AI system, model, framework, or application
licensed under this License.

"AI System" means any software that uses machine learning or other AI
techniques to make decisions, predictions, or recommendations.

"High-Stakes Decision" means a decision that has a significant impact on an
individual's legal rights, livelihood, health, or well-being.

"Bias Testing" means the process of evaluating an AI System for unfair or
discriminatory outcomes across different demographic groups.

"Human Oversight" means meaningful review of AI System outputs by a
qualified human before action is taken on High-Stakes Decisions.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software for any lawful
purpose, subject to the responsible AI conditions below.

### 2. Transparency.

If you deploy an AI System using the Software, you must publish a
transparency report that includes:
- **a)** The purpose and scope of the AI System;
- **b)** The data sources and training methodology;
- **c)** Known limitations and failure modes;
- **d)** Performance metrics across different demographic groups.

### 3. Bias Testing.

Before deploying an AI System that makes High-Stakes Decisions, you must:
- **a)** Conduct Bias Testing using representative data;
- **b)** Document the testing methodology and results;
- **c)** Mitigate identified biases where feasible;
- **d)** Disclose known biases that cannot be mitigated.

### 4. Human Oversight.

AI Systems that make High-Stakes Decisions must include Human Oversight
mechanisms:
- **a)** Humans must be able to review and override AI decisions;
- **b)** The AI System must explain its reasoning in understandable terms;
- **c)** Users must be informed when interacting with an AI System.

### 5. User Autonomy.

The AI System must:
- **a)** Allow users to opt out of AI-driven features where feasible;
- **b)** Provide meaningful choices about data collection and use;
- **c)** Not use dark patterns or manipulative design.

### 6. Termination.

Deployment of High-Stakes AI Systems without bias testing or human
oversight terminates rights.  A 90-day cure period applies.
