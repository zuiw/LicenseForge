## Preamble

The DTAL governs digital twin platforms that create virtual replicas of physical systems. It requires real-time synchronization between physical and digital entities, bidirectional data flow for control, and disclosure of model fidelity and limitations.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"Digital Twin" means a virtual representation of a physical system or process.

"Physical Twin" means the real-world entity mirrored by the Digital Twin.

"Fidelity" means the accuracy of the Digital Twin relative to the Physical Twin.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the digital twin conditions below.

### 2. Synchronization.

The Software must support real-time or near-real-time synchronization between Digital Twin and Physical Twin, with documented latency expectations.

### 3. Bidirectional Flow.

The Software must support data flow from physical to digital (sensing) and digital to physical (actuation), with safety interlocks for actuation.

### 4. Fidelity Disclosure.

You must document the Fidelity of the Digital Twin, including known deviations, update frequency, and data resolution limits.

### 5. Termination.
