## Preamble

The DTL governs the ethical trading of data in marketplaces. It requires provenance tracking to ensure data origin is known, fair compensation for data originators, and verification that consent was obtained. It aims to create a trustworthy data economy.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Data Trading License (DTL-1.0).

"The Software" means the data marketplace, exchange platform, or trading system licensed under this License.

"Data Asset" means a dataset or data stream offered for trade on the platform.

"Data Originator" means the person or entity from whom the Data Asset originates.

"Provenance Record" means a verifiable history of a Data Asset's origin and chain of custody.

"Consent" means explicit, informed permission to collect and trade the Data Asset.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the data trading conditions below.

### 2. Provenance Tracking.

Every Data Asset traded through the Software must have a Provenance Record including:
- **a)** Original source and collection method;
- **b)** Chain of custody through all intermediaries;
- **c)** Timestamp and cryptographic signature at each transfer;
- **d)** License or terms under which the data is offered.

### 3. Consent Verification.

Before a Data Asset is listed for trade, you must verify:
- **a)** That Consent was obtained from Data Originators;
- **b)** The scope and duration of Consent;
- **c)** Whether Consent can be withdrawn;
- **d)** Compliance with applicable data protection laws.

### 4. Fair Compensation.

The Software must:
- **a)** Disclose the compensation structure to Data Originators;
- **b)** Ensure Data Originators receive their agreed share;
- **c)** Provide transparent fee schedules;
- **d)** Support micropayments for per-use data access.

### 5. Data Quality.

Listings must include:
- **a)** Accuracy and completeness estimates;
- **b)** Known limitations or biases;
- **c)** Freshness/last update timestamp;
- **d)** Sample data for evaluation.

### 6. Termination.

Trading data without provenance or consent verification terminates rights. A 60-day cure period applies.
