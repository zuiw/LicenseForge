A license designed for cultural heritage institutions—museums, archives,

## Preamble

The CHL addresses the specific needs of cultural heritage institutions
digitizing and sharing their collections.  It maximizes public access to
cultural heritage while requiring attribution to the holding institution,
prohibiting misleading use of cultural materials, and encouraging users to
respect cultural sensitivities around sacred or culturally significant
items.

"The Institution" means the museum, archive, library, gallery, or other
cultural heritage institution that provides the Work.

"Cultural Sensitivity" means restrictions on use of certain items based on
their cultural, religious, or ceremonial significance to indigenous or

### 2. Conditions.

**a) Attribution.** You must credit the Institution that provided the Work.
The attribution must identify the Institution and, where applicable, the
original creator or community of origin.

**b) No Misleading Use.** You may not use the Work in a manner that
misrepresents the original cultural context, meaning, or significance of the
item.  Modified versions must be clearly identified as modifications.

**c) Cultural Respect.** Where the Institution has identified Cultural
Sensitivity concerns, you must respect those restrictions.  You may not use
the Work in a manner that would be offensive or derogatory to the community
of origin.

**d) Metadata Integrity.** You may not remove or alter metadata that
identifies the Work's provenance, cultural attribution, or usage
restrictions.

**e) Open Access Promotion.** If you distribute modified versions, you are
encouraged to contribute improvements back to the Institution for the
benefit of the broader cultural heritage community.

### 3. Institution Rights.

The Institution retains the right to impose additional use restrictions on
specific items where required by law, cultural protocols, or donor
