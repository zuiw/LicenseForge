## Preamble

The EDIL governs software for edge device identity, attestation, and authentication. It requires hardware-rooted trust where available, complete device lifecycle management (birth through decommissioning), and support for credential revocation.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"Edge Device" means a networked device outside a controlled data center.

"Device Identity" means a cryptographic credential binding the device to its identity.

"Attestation" means cryptographic proof of the device's software state.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the device identity conditions below.

### 2. Hardware Root of Trust.

Where hardware security modules are available, the Device Identity must be rooted in hardware.

### 3. Lifecycle Management.

The Software must support device provisioning, identity rotation, and secure decommissioning.

### 4. Revocation.

The Software must support credential revocation and provide mechanisms for distributing revocation information.

### 5. Termination.

Operating without revocation capability or with software-only identity where hardware is available terminates rights. A 60-day cure period applies.
