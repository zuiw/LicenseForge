A transitional license that automatically converts to a public domain
dedication after a defined abandonment period, designed for projects at
risk of becoming unmaintained.

## Preamble

The LAL addresses the problem of abandoned software: projects that are no
longer maintained but whose license terms prevent the community from taking
over.  Under the LAL, the software operates under permissive terms from the
start, but if the project becomes abandoned (no releases, no commits, no
response from maintainers for two years), it automatically enters the public
domain.  This ensures that abandoned projects can be revived without legal
uncertainty.
