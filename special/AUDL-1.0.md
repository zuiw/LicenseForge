## Preamble

The AUDL governs audit logging and monitoring systems. It requires tamper-evident log storage, real-time alerting on compliance violations, and support for forensic analysis in case of security incidents. It ensures that audit data can be trusted as evidence.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Audit License (AUDL-1.0).

"The Software" means the audit logging or compliance monitoring system licensed under this License.

"Audit Log" means a chronological record of system events relevant to compliance.

"Tamper-Evident" means that any modification to Audit Logs is detectable.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the audit conditions below.

### 2. Tamper-Evident Logs.

Audit Logs must be:
- **a)** Tamper-Evident using cryptographic chaining or similar mechanisms;
- **b)** Append-only with no deletion capability;
- **c)** Backed up to secure, separate storage.

### 3. Real-Time Alerting.

The Software must support configurable alerts for:
- **a)** Compliance policy violations;
- **b)** Unauthorized access attempts;
- **c)** Anomalous patterns in logged events;
- **d)** Audit system health failures.

### 4. Forensic Analysis.

The Software must support:
- **a)** Search and filter across all log fields;
- **b)** Timeline reconstruction of events;
- **c)** Correlation of related events across sources;
- **d)** Export of evidence packages for legal proceedings.

### 5. Log Retention.

Logs must be retained per configurable policy, with secure deletion after the retention period. Minimum retention must be documented.

### 6. Termination.

Operating without Tamper-Evident protection or with deletion capability terminates rights. No cure period for log tampering.
