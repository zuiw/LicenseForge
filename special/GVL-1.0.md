transparent and accountable governance structure.  It requires documented

sponsorship.

### 1. Permissions.

### 2. Governance Framework.

If you distribute the Software as a project lead or maintainer, you must
maintain a Governance Framework that documents:
- **a)** How decisions are made (consensus, voting, meritocracy);
- **b)** How Maintainers are added and removed;
- **c)** How disputes are resolved;
- **d)** How the project roadmap is determined.

### 3. Transparency.

The project must:
- **a)** Publish meeting notes or decision records;
- **b)** Maintain a public roadmap;
- **c)** Disclose all funding sources and sponsorship;
- **d)** Provide financial reports if accepting community funds.

### 4. Community Participation.

The Governance Framework must include:
- **a)** A code of conduct with enforcement procedures;
- **b)** A contributor onboarding process;
- **c)** Channels for community feedback and input;
- **d)** Regular community communications.

### 5. Governance Changes.

Changes to the Governance Framework must be:
- **a)** Proposed and discussed publicly;
- **b)** Subject to community review for at least 30 days;
- **c)** Approved through the process defined in the current framework.
