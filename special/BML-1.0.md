## Preamble

The BML governs software that processes biometric data for medical purposes (heart rate, ECG, blood oxygen, etc.). It requires disclosure of measurement accuracy under various conditions, bias testing across demographic populations, and strict protection of biometric health data.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Biometric Medical License (BML-1.0).

"The Software" means the biometric measurement or medical monitoring software licensed under this License.

"Biometric Data" means physiological measurements collected from a person's body.

"Accuracy" means the statistical agreement between the Software's measurements and a reference standard.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the biometric conditions below.

### 2. Accuracy Disclosure.

The Software must disclose:
- **a)** Accuracy metrics (MAE, RMSE, bias) against clinical reference standards;
- **b)** Conditions under which accuracy was measured;
- **c)** Known factors that reduce accuracy (motion, skin tone, ambient conditions);
- **d)** Intended use limitations.

### 3. Bias Testing.

The Software must be tested across demographic groups including:
- **a)** Skin tone variation (Fitzpatrick scale);
- **b)** Age ranges;
- **c)** Body mass index ranges;
- **d)** Gender;
- **e)** Results must be reported per group.

### 4. Data Protection.

Biometric Data must be:
- **a)** Encrypted at rest and in transit;
- **b)** Stored with access controls;
- **c)** Not shared with third parties without explicit consent;
- **d)** Deleted upon patient request.

### 5. Clinical Validation.

If the Software makes clinical claims, those claims must be supported by peer-reviewed validation studies.

### 6. Termination.

Concealing accuracy limitations or sharing biometric data without consent terminates rights. No cure for privacy violations.
