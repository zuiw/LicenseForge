A license that requires AI systems generating content using the software to
preserve attribution to original authors in their outputs.

## Preamble

The FAL addresses a critical challenge in the AI era: when AI systems
generate content, the original creators whose work influenced the output
deserve attribution.  This license permits full use of the software and its
training data, but requires that any AI output substantially derived from
or influenced by the licensed material include attribution to the original
authors.

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Fair Attribution License
(FAL-1.0).

"The Work" means the software, dataset, model, or creative work licensed
under this License.

"AI Output" means any content generated by an AI system that was trained
on or influenced by the Work.

"Substantial Influence" means the AI Output incorporates distinctive
elements, style, patterns, or information that are uniquely attributable
to the Work, as distinct from generic knowledge.

"Attribution Notice" means a clear statement crediting the original author
of the Work, including the author's name, the Work's title, and a link
to the original source where feasible.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Work for any lawful purpose,
including training AI models, subject to the attribution conditions below.

### 2. Training Permitted.

The Work may be used to train AI models.  This License explicitly permits
inclusion of the Work in training datasets.

### 3. Attribution in Output.

If an AI system trained on the Work generates AI Output that bears
Substantial Influence from the Work, the AI Output must include an
Attribution Notice.  The form of attribution should be appropriate to the
medium:
- **Text:** Include attribution in the text or metadata;
- **Images:** Include attribution in the image metadata or watermark;
- **Code:** Include attribution in comments or documentation;
- **Audio/Video:** Include attribution in credits or metadata.

### 4. Attribution Standard.

The Attribution Notice must include:
- The name of the author or copyright holder;
- The title of the Work;
- A link to the original source (where feasible);
- A statement that the Work is licensed under FAL-1.0.

### 5. No False Attribution.

You may not falsely attribute AI Output to a human author when it was
AI-generated, even if attribution to the Work is included.

### 6. Termination.

period applies for a first violation.
