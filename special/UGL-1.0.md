A license for platforms hosting user-generated content requiring creator ownership, content moderation transparency, and fair dispute resolution.

## Preamble

The UGL governs platforms that host content created by users. It affirms that UGC remains the property of the creator, requires transparent content moderation policies, and provides fair dispute resolution mechanisms. It prevents platforms from exploiting user creativity while maintaining safe communities.

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the User-Generated Content License (UGL-1.0).

"The Software" means the UGC platform or content hosting system licensed under this License.

"User Content" means any content uploaded, created, or shared by a user on the platform.

"Moderation Action" means removal, restriction, or flagging of User Content.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the UGC conditions below.

### 2. Creator Ownership.

User Content remains the property of the user who created it. The platform may not claim ownership of User Content. A license from the user to the platform for operational purposes must be limited to what is necessary to provide the service.

### 3. Content Moderation.

Moderation Actions must be:
- **a)** Based on published, specific policies;
- **b)** Applied consistently across similar content;
- **c)** Reversible on appeal;
- **d)** Notified to the affected user with explanation.

### 4. Dispute Resolution.

You must provide:
- **a)** A clear appeals process for Moderation Actions;
- **b)** Human review for appealed decisions;
- **c)** Timely response within 14 days;
- **d)** Final escalation to independent arbitration.

### 5. Data Portability.

Users must be able to export their User Content in a standard format at any time.

### 6. Termination.

Claiming ownership of User Content or denying meaningful appeal terminates rights. A 30-day cure period applies.
