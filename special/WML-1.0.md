## Preamble

The WML governs software that embeds or detects digital watermarks in content. It requires disclosure of watermark robustness, false positive/negative rates, and protection against removal. It ensures that watermarking is applied transparently and can be relied upon for provenance and copyright enforcement.

## TERMS AND CONDITIONS

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Digital Watermarking License (WML-1.0).

"The Software" means the watermarking or fingerprinting system licensed under this License.

"Watermark" means an imperceptible signal embedded in content for identification.

"Robustness" means the watermark's resistance to removal, modification, or transformation.

### 1. Permissions.

You may use, copy, modify, and distribute the Software, subject to the watermarking conditions below.

### 2. Transparency.

You must document:
- **a)** The watermarking algorithm used;
- **b)** Robustness against common transformations (compression, cropping, resizing);
- **c)** False positive and false negative rates;
- **d)** Capacity limits (bits of data embeddable).

### 3. Non-Removal.

The Software must not intentionally create watermarks that are easily removable or that degrade content quality beyond stated parameters.

### 4. Integrity.

If the Software detects watermarks, it must provide confidence scores and disclose detection limitations.

### 5. Privacy.

Watermarking must not be used for covert surveillance or user tracking beyond the content itself.

### 6. Termination.

Misrepresenting watermark robustness or using for covert surveillance terminates rights. No cure for surveillance violations.
