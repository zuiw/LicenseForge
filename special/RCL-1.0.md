A copyleft license that requires commercial users to share a percentage of

## Preamble

The RCL establishes a sustainable funding model for open-source projects.
Non-commercial users and small businesses enjoy full copyleft rights at no
cost.  Larger commercial users must share a small percentage of the revenue
they derive from the software with the project's maintenance fund.

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Revenue Copyleft License
(RCL-1.0).

"The Software" means the program licensed under this License.

"Revenue Threshold" means $5,000,000 USD in gross annual revenue from all
sources, calculated on the most recently completed fiscal year for your
entire organization.

"Applicable Revenue" means the gross revenue directly attributable to
products or services that incorporate or depend on the Software.

"Revenue Share Percentage" means 2% of Applicable Revenue, or such lower
percentage as the copyright holder may designate in the License notice.

"Project Fund" means a fund designated by the copyright holder for the
ongoing maintenance and development of the Software.

### 1. Full Rights Below Threshold.

If your organization's revenue is below the Revenue Threshold, you are
granted full copyleft rights: you may use, copy, modify, and distribute
the Software, provided that modified versions are licensed under this
License.

### 2. Revenue Share Above Threshold.

If your organization's revenue equals or exceeds the Revenue Threshold, you
must additionally:
- **a)** Pay the Revenue Share Percentage to the Project Fund on an annual
  basis;
- **b)** Provide a written accounting of Applicable Revenue to the Project
  Fund.

### 3. Revenue Cap.

The maximum annual revenue share obligation for any single licensee is
$500,000 USD per year.

### 4. Verification.

The Project Fund may request a third-party audit of your Applicable Revenue
calculation no more than once every three years.  If the audit reveals a
underpayment of more than 10%, you must pay the cost of the audit.

### 5. Termination.

Failure to pay the required revenue share terminates all rights.  A 90-day
cure period applies for a first violation.
