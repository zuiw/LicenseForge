A license designed for indigenous cultural knowledge, traditional ecological
knowledge, and culturally significant data, ensuring community consent and
benefit-sharing.

## Preamble

The INL implements the CARE Principles for Indigenous Data Governance
(Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics).  It
recognizes that indigenous communities have sovereign rights over their
cultural knowledge and data.  Use of INL-licensed materials requires
community consent, respects traditional protocols, and ensures that
benefits from use flow back to the community.

### 0. Definitions.

"This License" refers to version 1.0 of the Indigenous Knowledge License

"The Knowledge" means the indigenous cultural knowledge, traditional
ecological knowledge, data, recordings, or materials licensed under this
License.

"The Community" means the indigenous community, nation, or people that is
the source of the Knowledge and identified in the License notice.

"Community Consent" means free, prior, and informed consent from the
Community's designated governing body, following the Community's own
protocols and decision-making processes.

"Cultural Protocol" means the Community's customary laws, protocols, and
practices regarding the use, reproduction, and dissemination of the
Knowledge.

### 1. Grant.

Subject to the conditions below, you may use, copy, and study the Knowledge
for non-commercial research and educational purposes.

### 2. Community Consent.

**a)** Any use beyond non-commercial research requires Community Consent.
**b)** Commercial use requires a separate benefit-sharing agreement with the
  Community.
**c)** Publishing or distributing the Knowledge requires Community Consent.

### 3. Cultural Protocols.

You must respect and follow Cultural Protocols regarding:
- Sacred or restricted knowledge that should not be publicly shared;
- Proper attribution and acknowledgment of the Community;
- Seasonal or ceremonial restrictions on access;
- Gender-restricted knowledge.

### 4. Attribution.

You must attribute the Knowledge to the Community using the attribution
format specified by the Community.

### 5. Benefit Sharing.

If you derive commercial value from the Knowledge, you must negotiate a
fair and equitable benefit-sharing agreement with the Community.

### 6. Derivative Knowledge.

Derived works that incorporate the Knowledge remain subject to Community
Consent requirements for any use that could affect the Community's
interests.

### 7. Termination.
